The Hearthside Story

Crafting the Art of the Slow.

"A restoration of time, stone, and silence in the heart of the Gloucestershire countryside."

From Coaching Inn
to Country Retreat

The stones of Hearthside have witnessed two centuries of Gloucestershire history. Originally built as 'The Travellers Rest' coaching inn in 1824, the property served as a vital waypoint for merchants and wanderers crossing the Cotswolds.

For decades, it was the beating heart of the village—a place of laughter, local news, and roaring fires. When we acquired the property five years ago, it had fallen into silence. Our mission was simple but daunting: to restore the craftsmanship of the past while providing the comfort of the present.

Today, Hearthside stands as a boutique retreat that honors its roots. Every beam has been treated, every sash window liberated, and every fireplace brought back to life.

Craftsmanship

We favor the handmade over the mass-produced. From the hand-turned wooden bowls in our dining room to the traditional lime plaster on our walls, we celebrate the work of the human hand.

Locality

We are deeply embedded in our soil. We source our breakfast from within ten miles, our garden is planted with native wildflowers, and our guides are written by people who live here.

Hospitality

To us, luxury isn't about excess; it's about being known. We remember how you like your coffee, where you like to walk, and ensure your fireplace is lit before you return.

Your Hosts,
Catherine & Julian.

After twenty years in the busy culinary world of London, we came to the Cotswolds looking for a different pace of life. Catherine is a pastry chef by training and the heart behind the Hearthside breakfast. Julian is an architect with a passion for heritage restoration.

"We treated this house like our own home before we ever opened it to guests. We want you to feel that same sense of belonging from the moment you turn onto Mill Lane."
